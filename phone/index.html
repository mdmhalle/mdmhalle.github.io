Matthieu (Donald Maurice) Hallé is a video artist currently living in Ottawa, ON. He performs live visual shows, and is the director of various short videos, as well as the feature length, Margraue (2013). He is mindful of his birth in Chilliwack, BC, his nomadic upbringing, studying film in Montréal, and farming in the summers.

visual shows

UNTITLED (broken glass and fire)

A live and adaptive performance technique I developed to make creating and improvising visuals feel for me more like playing a musical instrument.

 

Candle light is focused with broken pieces of crystal onto a video sensor that outputs directly to a projector.

Club Saw, Ottawa, ON - April 12th, 2015

Fet.Nat. live at Le Temporaire, Hull, QC, September, 5th, 2015

SYNESTHESIA

A live audio/visual performance collaboration between myself and musician Adam Saikaley commissioned by SAW video.

 

The piece is inspired by cymatics (the study of visible sound). For this performance, a tank of water with a mirrored surface is placed on top of a subwoofer playing the live music. Depending on the specific frequency, different nodal patterns emerge in the water. The video is projected through the water and reflected onto a screen resulting in a final image that is distorted by the ripple patterns.

 

This was performed August 1st, 2014 at St. Alban's church, Ottawa, ON.
